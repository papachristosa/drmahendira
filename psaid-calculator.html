Psoriatic arthritis · Self-tracking tool

PsAID: track the impact of your disease

The Psoriatic Arthritis Impact of Disease (PsAID-12) is a twelve-question tool that captures how psoriatic arthritis has affected you physically and emotionally over the last week, pain, fatigue, skin, function, sleep, mood, and more. Complete it, save your result to track it over time, and print it to bring to your rheumatology appointment.

About the scale. A PsAID score under 4.0 is generally considered a patient-acceptable symptom state (Gossec et al, 2014). The 12 domains carry different weights in the formula, pain and fatigue-related domains count for more, reflecting how much patients said each mattered to them when the tool was developed. Treat any single score as one data point for a conversation with your team, not a diagnosis or a treatment decision on its own.

How the score is calculated

PsAID-12 = (3×Pain + 2×Fatigue + 2×Skin + 2×Work/leisure + 2×Function + 2×Discomfort + 2×Sleep + Coping + Anxiety + Embarrassment + Social participation + Feeling low) ÷ 20. Higher-weighted domains (pain, fatigue, skin, function, discomfort, sleep) count for more than the psychosocial domains, reflecting their relative importance to patients.

Gossec L, de Wit M, Kiltz U, et al, on behalf of the EULAR PsAID Taskforce. A patient-derived and patient-reported outcome measure for assessing psoriatic arthritis: elaboration and preliminary validation of the Psoriatic Arthritis Impact of Disease (PsAID) questionnaire, a 13-country EULAR initiative. Ann Rheum Dis. 2014;73(6):1012 to 1019.
