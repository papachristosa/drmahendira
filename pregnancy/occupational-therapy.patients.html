Occupational therapy

OT, occupational therapist

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionAn occupational therapist helps you keep doing daily tasks with less strain on your joints and energy, during pregnancy and once the baby arrives. That includes joint protection, pacing your energy, hand supports, and setting up feeding and baby care so they are easier on you.
  • OT is about keeping daily life doable with less strain, before and after birth.

What this involves

Joint protection

Simple changes to how you lift, carry, and grip protect sore or swollen joints during pregnancy and baby care.

Read the full guide

Energy and pacing

Fatigue is common. An OT helps you plan and pace tasks so you have energy for what matters, including nights with a newborn.

Read the full guide

Hands and splinting

Hand and wrist problems such as carpal tunnel are common in pregnancy. Splints and technique changes can ease symptoms.

Read the full guide

Setting up for baby care

An OT can help set up feeding positions, carriers, and equipment so caring for your baby is easier on your joints and back.

Read the full guide

When to call your team

  • New numbness or weakness in the hand that is severe or persistent.
  • General pregnancy red flags on your condition page.
  • Ask for a referral if daily tasks are becoming difficult or painful.
