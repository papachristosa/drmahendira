For patients

Cyclophosphamide

CYC, Cytoxan

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionCyclophosphamide is avoided in early pregnancy because it can harm a developing baby, and it can affect fertility, so your team will discuss protecting your fertility before treatment. It is only considered later in pregnancy if a mother has severe, life-threatening disease.
  • Cyclophosphamide can affect fertility, so plan ahead with your team.

For fathers-to-be

Cyclophosphamide can cause lasting infertility in men, so sperm banking is offered before starting. It is not thought to harm a future baby through the father.

iThis page helps you prepare a conversation with your care team. It does not replace individual medical advice. Always confirm your own plan with your rheumatology and pregnancy team.
