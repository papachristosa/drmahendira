For patients

Low-dose aspirin

ASA, baby aspirin, acetylsalicylic acid 81mg

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionLow-dose aspirin is often recommended in pregnancy to lower the chance of pre-eclampsia, especially if you have lupus or antiphospholipid antibodies. It is different from regular-dose anti-inflammatory painkillers and is considered compatible through pregnancy and breastfeeding.
  • Low-dose aspirin is used to lower the chance of pre-eclampsia if you are at higher risk.

Through your pregnancy journey

Use if needed

Before pregnancy

May be started when planning if you are at higher risk; your team will advise.

Continue

First trimester

Usually started in the first trimester if you have lupus or antiphospholipid antibodies.

Continue

Second & third trimester

Usually continued through pregnancy (often to around 36 weeks or as advised).

Continue

Breastfeeding

Low-dose aspirin is considered compatible with breastfeeding.

Continue

For fathers-to-be

Not relevant to fathers for pregnancy; compatible generally.

iThis page helps you prepare a conversation with your care team. It does not replace individual medical advice. Always confirm your own plan with your rheumatology and pregnancy team.
!

When to call your team

  • Confirm your aspirin plan with your team; do not confuse low-dose aspirin with regular painkillers.
  • Report any unusual bleeding.
