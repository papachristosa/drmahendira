For patients

NSAIDs

ibuprofen, naproxen, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionNSAIDs like ibuprofen can be used for short periods in early and mid pregnancy if needed, but are stopped in the later part of pregnancy because they can affect the baby's circulation and the fluid around the baby. If you are trying to conceive, heavy use can make it harder to fall pregnant.
  • Stop NSAIDs in the last part of pregnancy unless your team specifically advises otherwise.
  • If you are trying to conceive, heavy NSAID use may make it harder.

Used only now and then earlier in pregnancy, and stopped after 28 weeks.

When to call your team

  • Stop NSAIDs in the later part of pregnancy unless your team advises otherwise.
  • Ask your team what to use for pain relief in the third trimester.
  • Mention NSAID use if you are having difficulty conceiving.
