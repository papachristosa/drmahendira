Mixed and undifferentiated connective tissue disease

MCTD, mixed connective tissue disease, UCTD, undifferentiated connective tissue disease

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionThese conditions have overlapping features of lupus, scleroderma, and myositis. Your pregnancy plan is tailored to your particular mix of features. Two things your team checks for are anti-Ro/SSA antibodies (which affect baby's heart monitoring) and, in some people, lung artery pressure.
  • Your care is guided by your specific features, not just the diagnosis name.

What to know

A plan tailored to your features

Because these conditions look different from person to person, your plan depends on which features you have, such as joints, skin, muscle, or internal organs.

Anti-Ro/SSA antibodies

If you carry anti-Ro/SSA antibodies, your pregnancy includes monitoring of the baby's heart, and hydroxychloroquine helps.

Lung artery pressure

Because these conditions can overlap with scleroderma, your team may check the pressure in your lung arteries before pregnancy.

Medicines

Hydroxychloroquine is usually continued. Other medicines depend on your features and are chosen to suit pregnancy.

iThis page helps you prepare a conversation with your care team. It does not replace individual medical advice. Always confirm your own plan with your rheumatology and pregnancy team.
!

When to call your team

  • New breathlessness or chest pain.
  • New or worsening rash, muscle weakness, or joint swelling.
  • General pregnancy red flags (bleeding, reduced movements, severe headache).
