For patients

Methotrexate

MTX

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionMethotrexate is stopped before pregnancy because it can harm a developing baby. Use reliable contraception while taking it. For men hoping to father a child, recent evidence is reassuring about low-dose methotrexate, though older advice was to stop it first; discuss your own situation with your team.
  • Do not take methotrexate if there is any chance you could be pregnant.
  • For fathers-to-be, low-dose methotrexate now appears compatible on current evidence.

Continue

For fathers-to-be

Recent evidence suggests low-dose methotrexate is compatible for fathers-to-be, though older advice was to stop it for 3 months. Discuss with your team.

iThis page helps you prepare a conversation with your care team. It does not replace individual medical advice. Always confirm your own plan with your rheumatology and pregnancy team.
!

When to call your team

  • Use reliable contraception while taking methotrexate.
  • If you think you may be pregnant while taking methotrexate, contact your team promptly.
  • Do not start trying to conceive until your team confirms it is safe to stop.
