Trusted resources and support

Our own guides go deep on the conditions we treat, but there is a wider world of good, reputable information and support out there. These are organisations, programs, and videos we consider trustworthy starting points. Use them alongside our pages, and alongside advice from your own care team.

All patient guidesCommon questions
iThese are independent organisations and resources, not part of this practice. We choose them carefully, but we do not control their content, and nothing here replaces advice from the team who knows your situation. Links open in a new tab.

Start here: arthritis and rheumatology organisations

General, reputable information on rheumatic disease and its treatment. The Canadian ones come first, since coverage, programs, and terminology match your care here most closely.

Canada

Arthritis Society Canada

Canada's national arthritis charity: condition guides, exercise and pain resources, online learning, and a funded rehabilitation program.

Visit siteCanada

Canadian Rheumatology Association

The national body of Canadian rheumatologists, with a hub of vetted patient resources.

Visit siteCanada

RheumInfo

Plain-language summaries of rheumatic diseases, medications, and lab tests by a Canadian rheumatologist. Available in several languages.

Visit siteCanada

Canadian Arthritis Patient Alliance

A patient-led group giving people with arthritis a voice in research, policy, and care.

Visit siteReference

American College of Rheumatology

Clear patient fact sheets on conditions and medications from the largest US rheumatology society.

Visit siteReference

Arthritis Foundation (US)

US arthritis charity with living-well guides, nutrition advice, and community resources.

Visit siteReference

Versus Arthritis (UK)

UK arthritis charity known for clear, well-designed booklets on conditions and staying active.

Pregnancy, breastfeeding, and family

Rheumatic disease and its medicines raise specific questions around conception, pregnancy, and breastfeeding. Start with our own clinic pages, and use these for individual medication questions and lactation.

Our clinic

Our RheumPreg clinic

Our own in-depth, sourced guidance on rheumatic disease before, during, and after pregnancy, for patients and clinicians.

Open guidePregnancy

MotherToBaby

Fact sheets and a free helpline on the safety of specific medications in pregnancy and breastfeeding, plus a lactation app. The accessible replacement for the former Toronto MotherRisk service.

Visit sitePregnancy

HopStep (lupus and pregnancy)

A patient guide to a healthy pregnancy with lupus, developed by Duke University.

Ontario programs you can actually use

These are real services for people in Ontario, several of them free or OHIP-funded, covering exercise, pain, and self-management. Some need a referral; ask us or your family doctor if one interests you.

Ontario / Canada

GLA:D Canada

A group education and exercise program for hip and knee osteoarthritis, delivered by trained therapists.

Visit siteOntario

Arthritis Rehabilitation & Education Program

Free virtual physiotherapy and occupational therapy for people with arthritis, funded for OHIP-insured Ontarians.

Visit siteOntario

TAPMI: Pain U Online

Free online learning from the Toronto Academic Pain Medicine Institute on understanding and managing chronic pain.

Visit siteOntario / Canada

Power Over Pain Portal

Free tools, short courses, and peer support for living with chronic pain.

Visit siteOntario

SelfManagement Ontario

Free workshops across Ontario for living well with a chronic condition, in person or virtual.

Visit siteOntario

Health811

Ontario's free, confidential health advice line and service finder, by phone (call 811) or online.
