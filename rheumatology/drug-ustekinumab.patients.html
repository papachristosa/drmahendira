Ustekinumab

anti-IL-12/23, Stelara

Are you a clinician? See the clinical version of this page.

The short versionUstekinumab is a biologic medicine that blocks two inflammation signals, IL-12 and IL-23. It is used in psoriatic arthritis and psoriasis, and can help both the joints and the skin.
How you take it

Given as an injection under the skin: a dose at the start and 4 weeks later, then every 12 weeks. It blocks IL-12 and IL-23 and is used mainly for psoriatic arthritis. You will be checked for tuberculosis first.

  • Psoriatic arthritis: An injection at week 0 and week 4, then every 12 weeks.

Tests while you take it

You are checked for tuberculosis before starting. Report signs of infection.

What to know

Common side effects

The most common effects are reactions where the injection is given and a higher chance of infections. It is generally well tolerated.

Tests and monitoring

Before starting, your team checks for hidden infections such as tuberculosis. While on treatment, tell your team about infections.

Important cautions

As with other medicines that calm the immune system, infections need watching, and live vaccines are timed with your team.

What to watch for

  • Signs of infection with a fever, or an infection that does not clear.
  • Feeling generally unwell in a way that does not settle.
