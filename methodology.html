How we make this

Methodology, evidence, and sign-off

Why this site can be trusted, and how it is kept accurate. Written for patients and clinicians alike.

One source of truth, two doors

Each clinical topic is a single reviewed record. That one record renders two pages: a plain-language page for patients and a sourced page for clinicians. Because both come from the same record, the two audiences can never be shown contradictory facts.

Every claim is sourced

Clinical statements are checked against primary guidelines and studies, read line by line, not written from memory. Where guidelines disagree, we show each position (ACR, EULAR, BSR) and flag the divergence rather than quietly picking one. Medication guidance is never stated in categorical "safe" or "unsafe" terms; it is framed by phase and always defers to the treating team.

Verification status

Every page shows how far it has been checked

Verified

Guideline positions checked verbatim against the primary source tables.

Partly verified

Part of the record is confirmed against primary text; the rest is flagged for review.

In review

Drafted from standard references, not yet checked against primary text. Not published.

Clinician sign-off

No clinical page, drug position, or evidence grade is published until Dr. Mahendira has reviewed and signed it off. She is the sole clinical authority for this site. Every clinical page carries a visible "last reviewed" date and a dated changelog so you can see what changed and when.

Kept current

We track the guideline update cycle (ACR, EULAR, BSR) and the relevant trial pipeline, and refresh the fast-moving pages on a schedule. When a source moves, the record moves, and both the patient and clinician pages update together.

Disclaimer. This site is for education and to help prepare a conversation with your healthcare team. It does not replace individual medical advice, and it never tells anyone to start or stop a medication. Always confirm decisions with your treating clinician.
