Why these clinics exist

Rheumatic disease is complex. Care for it should not feel improvised.

Inflammatory and autoimmune disease rarely stays in one place. It can arrive with a pregnancy, settle into a single joint, or move through the whole body, and the questions it raises often fall between clinics: is this medication safe to continue while trying to conceive? Is an injection worth trying before surgery?

Too often those questions meet a "wait and see," not because anyone is failing the patient, but because the ordinary clinic is not built around them. A patient weighing pregnancy against active disease, or surgery against a joint that might still respond, needs a team that has seen the pattern before and knows what comes next.

Dr. Mahendira's clinics are built for exactly that. Reproductive rheumatology, complex osteoarthritis, and general inflammatory disease each have a rheumatologist-led team and a defined approach, working from one record, so the plan is deliberate from the first visit rather than pieced together over years.
